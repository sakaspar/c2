# Salafni Business Model Canvas (BMC)

## KEY PARTNERS
**Who are the key partners/suppliers?**
- **Local Retailers & Merchants:** Physical and e-commerce stores in Tunisia that integrate Salafni as a payment method.
- **Financial Institutions:** Partner banks for liquidity, credit lines, and regulatory compliance.
- **Payment Gateways:** Local providers like GIM-Tel or Monétique Tunisie for processing initial installments and repayments.
- **Identity Verification Services:** Providers for automated CIN and document verification (KYC).
- **Cloud Infrastructure Providers:** AWS/Google Cloud for hosting the platform and the JSON data lake.

**Do we engage with any consulting firms?**
- Yes, legal and compliance firms specialized in Tunisian fintech regulations and micro-credit laws.

**Which key resources or capabilities do they provide?**
- Compliance with Central Bank of Tunisia (BCT) regulations.
- Access to a diverse customer base through merchant storefronts.
- Technical stability and secure payment processing.

**Why do you work with these partners?**
- To reduce operational risk, ensure legal compliance, and leverage existing retail traffic for rapid scaling without massive direct marketing spend.

---

## KEY ACTIVITIES
**What are the key processes/tasks you need to create to deliver the value proposition?**
- **Credit Scoring:** Developing and refining algorithms for dynamic credit limit calculation.
- **Merchant Onboarding:** Efficient KYB (Know Your Business) processes to expand the network.
- **KYC/Risk Management:** Continuous monitoring of applications to prevent fraud.
- **Platform Development:** maintaining the Next.js/NestJS tech stack and the filesystem-based data lake.
- **Collection Management:** Automating weekly installment reminders and handling late repayments.

**What do you develop, sell or manage?**
- We develop the BNPL API and web interfaces.
- We "sell" financial flexibility and increased purchasing power.
- We manage the relationship between the buyer, the merchant, and the loan lifecycle.

**What makes you do it better than our competitors?**
- **Hyper-Local Focus:** Tailored to the Tunisian market (TND currency, local CIN verification).
- **Speed:** Rapid approval workflows for immediate "in-store" or "at-checkout" credit.
- **Transparency:** Clear 4-weekly installment structure with minimal hidden fees compared to traditional bank loans.

**Do you need to streamline to keep costs and prices low?**
- Yes, particularly the KYC and scoring processes. Automating these reduces the need for expensive risk analyst manual reviews, allowing us to offer lower fees to merchants and users.

---

## VALUE PROPOSITION
**What is the critical problem you are trying to solve?**
- Limited access to traditional credit for the "unbanked" or "under-banked" population in Tunisia.
- Cash flow management for consumers facing large one-time expenses.

**What functional, emotional and ethical benefits do you deliver?**
- **Functional:** Instant credit at the point of sale; simple weekly repayment schedule.
- **Emotional:** Peace of mind, financial empowerment, and the ability to afford quality goods today.
- **Ethical:** Transparent terms that avoid the "debt trap" associated with high-interest revolving credit.

**Are there multiple problems you are trying to solve?**
- Yes: Consumer purchasing power and Merchant sales conversion/average order value (AOV).

**How does your VP satisfy the demands of your customer segments?**
- It provides immediate gratification for consumers and immediate revenue for merchants, bridging the gap between desire and affordability.

## KEY RESOURCES
**What are the key assets or resources needed to deliver your value proposition?**
- **Proprietary Credit Scoring Engine:** The algorithm that determines risk tiers.
- **Tech Stack:** The NestJS/Next.js codebase and the JSON data lake storage system.
- **Capital Reserve:** Funds required to pay merchants upfront before consumers repay Salafni.
- **Brand Identity:** Trust and recognition in the Tunisian retail market.

**Do you have any key partners to deliver the critical activities?**
- Partner merchants are critical for the "checkout" activity.
- Cloud providers are critical for "platform availability."

**What resources do you need to maintain strong customer relationships?**
- Customer support dashboard (Admin Command Center).
- Automated notification system (SMS/Email) for reminders and transparency.

- Through transparency, proactive notifications about upcoming payments, and a seamless mobile-responsive dashboard.

- By providing a dedicated Merchant Portal, timely settlements, and data insights into their customers' buying habits.

**How do you communicate to your audiences?**

**How much support do you provide to your audiences?**

## CUSTOMER SEGMENTS
**Who is your ideal customer profile (ICP)?**
- Young professionals and middle-income families in Tunisia (ages 20-45) who are digitally savvy but may lack access to premium banking credit cards.

**Who are your most important customers?**
- "Active" users who repay on time and "Approved" merchants with high transaction volumes.

**What do your customers want, need, enjoy?**
- They want quality electronics, fashion, and home goods without draining their entire monthly salary in one day.

**What's happening in your customer market?**
- Increasing shift toward e-commerce and a rising demand for flexible payment alternatives amidst inflation.

**Are you targeting a niche community or mass audience?**
- Starting with a niche (urban tech-savvy users) with the goal of scaling to a mass audience across Tunisia.

## COST STRUCTURE
**What are the key costs in your business model?**
- **Cost of Capital:** The cost of borrowing or allocating funds to front the loans.
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** Marketing and merchant partnership incentives.
- **Technical Maintenance:** Hosting, security audits, and developer salaries.
- **Risk/Bad Debt:** The cost of defaults where users fail to repay.

**What are the major drivers of your costs?**
- Default rates and the scale of the merchant network.

**How do your key activities and key resources contribute to your cost structure?**
- The efficiency of the Credit Scoring Activity directly reduces the "Bad Debt" cost.
- The Cloud Resource cost scales with the "User/Transaction" volume.

**How do your costs relate to revenue streams?**
- Fixed costs (Dev/Staff) are covered by the volume of transactions, while variable costs (Bad Debt/Processing) are managed per transaction.

**What proportions of costs are fixed and variable?**
- Fixed: ~40% (Staff, Infrastructure).
- Variable: ~60% (Capital costs, bad debt, transaction processing).

**Are you focused on cost optimization or value?**
- Value-driven initially to build the brand, transitioning to cost-optimization (automation) as volume increases.

## CHANNELS
**How do you deliver your value proposition?**
- Integrated "Pay with Salafni" buttons on merchant websites.
- Direct-to-consumer Salafni Dashboard for account management.

**How do you reach your customer segments?**
- Point-of-sale marketing in physical stores.
- Affiliate marketing through merchant newsletters.
- Digital marketing targeted at Tunisian consumers.

**How integrated are your channels in the supply chain?**
- Highly integrated at the "Payment" stage of the retail supply chain.

**How cost-efficient are your channels?**
- Merchant integration is highly efficient as it uses the partner's existing traffic to acquire users.

**Are your channels being utilised efficiently?**
- Yes, by focusing on high-traffic merchants first to maximize visibility.

## REVENUE STREAMS
**What ways does your value proposition generate money for your business?**
- **Merchant Commission:** Charging retailers a percentage (e.g., 3-6%) for every successful Salafni transaction.
- **Late Fees:** Fixed charges for missed weekly installments (structured ethically).
- **Service Fees:** Potential small flat fee per loan for consumers (optional).

**Do we have multiple ways of generating revenue?**
- Yes: B2B (Merchant fees) and B2C (Late fees/Service charges).

**What is your pricing strategy for the products/services you offer?**
- Value-based pricing for merchants (charging for the increase in sales they otherwise wouldn't have).

**Through what channels do your customers pay?**
- Direct bank transfer, credit/debit card via the dashboard, or potentially cash-at-merchant for the first installment.

**Do you offer multiple forms of payment?**
- Yes, we aim to support all major local payment methods for repayments.
